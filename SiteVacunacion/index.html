Doctors have long known that excessive alcohol consumption — more than four drinks on a given day for men or more than three for women — can do a number on the immune system. Not only do heavy drinkers recover from infection and wound-healing more slowly than their teetotaling counterparts, they're also more susceptible to pneumonia and at higher risk for both bacterial and viral infections and a range of medical conditions, including acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS), sepsis, alcoholic liver disease and certain cancers.

Stress has a profound effect on immune work. Moreover, prolonged stress can increare cortison production and oxidative stress in the dody.

To help you with some answers, Dr Uma Naidoo, Harvard Nutritional Psychiatrist, recently took to Instagram and said: “It is so important to take into account your diet around when you get the vaccine to reduce any side effects you may get.”

Several studies have found an association between sleep and vaccination response. For example, a 2020 study in the International Journal of Behavioral Medicine found that flu vaccines appear to be more effective in people who get a sufficient amount of sleep during the two nights prior to receiving the shot. Other studies have reported similar findings about patients' response to vaccines for hepatitis A and hepatitis B.
